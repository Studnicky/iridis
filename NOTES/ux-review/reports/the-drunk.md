# The Drunk — blind first look at some glowy color-machine thing called IRIDIS

## Who I am
Four drinks in, phone in one hand, and if the button isn't the size of my face I'm gone.

## My first five seconds
Okay so — WHOA. Black space, little stars floating, and there's this spinny rainbow eyeball donut thing in the middle that I honestly stared at for way too long. Pretty. Real pretty. Then giant chunky letters, **IRIDIS**, big and bold, love that, I can read THAT even sideways. Good. Good start.

BUT. Before I even got to the pretty part my eyes hit this WALL of tiny tabs at the top. Like forty little words all screaming at once — UPLOAD, COMBINE, MANUAL, PALETTE, CVD VISION, SCHEMA AND COMPLI-something, RDF TURTLE?? Turtle? Whatssa turtle doing here. It's a whole menu the size of a menu at a diner that has too many pancakes. My eyes crossed. I scrolled away from it fast.

## What I think this is / who it's for
Okay here's my honest drunk guess: it's a machine where you throw in a picture and it barfs out a buncha matching colors for, like, websites or apps. There's a big purple **UPLOAD** button and it says something about "any image" turning into a "palette." So — color robot. For computer people. The nerdy kind, the ones who say "hex" and "OKLCH" (what is that, I don't know, sounds like a cough) and stare at little slidey bars all day. NOT for me, a person holding a beer. But the rainbow donut? That's for everybody. That's art.

## Where my eyes went & what I'd tap first
The big purple UPLOAD button. Obviously. It glows, it's got a little arrow, it's the one thing that looks tappable and friendly. Ten outta ten, I found the button, I'd smash it.

There's also a little toggle — LIGHT / DARK — with a cute lil moon on it, and a dropdown that says "Futuristic." I don't know what Futuristic does but I'd poke it just to see. Fun.

Everything ELSE though? I have no idea where to go. That top tab-wall isn't wayfinding, it's a phone book. I wouldn't tap any of those on purpose, my thumb's too fat and there's too many.

## What confused, annoyed, or lost me
- The tab-wall. Again. It NEVER GOES AWAY. On the phone it's stuck to the top like gum on a shoe, eating a third of my screen the WHOLE time. Every scroll, still there. Let me breathe.
- Screen with all the sliders (screenshot 01) — "Clustering algorithm," "Merge input cap 128," "Harmonize threshold," and then a PARAGRAPH. A whole gray paragraph explaining O(n²) something. Nope. My eyes slid right off it like a wet bar stool. Too many words, too small, all the same mushy gray.
- The one that's just green code on black (screenshot 05 / phone 06) — `--c-background: #060102` on and on and ON. That's not a website that's the Matrix. I felt like I broke something.
- That "1. intake:hexHint / 2. derive:semanticHues / 3. resolve:roles" list. Sounds like a robot reciting a grocery list. Twelve of 'em. Twelve!
- Some middle screens have all this GHOSTLY faded text bleeding in behind the main box (screenshot 03), like two pages printed on top of each other. Made me feel more drunk than I actually am. Dizzy.

## What I actually liked
- The rainbow donut eyeball. Say it with me. Beautiful. I'd frame it.
- The floaty star dust in the background — twinkly, chill, didn't get in my way.
- ALL the little color squares! The grids of colored blocks (the SAMPLE one, screenshot 02, and that big roles table screenshot 04) — those I liked. Colors are shiny, colors are fun, my drunk brain goes "ooh" at a wall of colored tiles even if I can't read the tiny words under 'em. Candy for the eyes.
- Big chunky headers — IRIDIS, EXPLORE, REFINE, COMBINE — those glowy title words are HUGE and readable and I always knew a new section started. That rhythm, thumbs up.
- The purple buttons glow like they want to be pressed. Good button.

## On my phone vs the big screen
Big screen's better, not gonna lie. On the computer the color squares line up in neat wide rows and there's black empty space around stuff so my eyes get a rest. Roomy. The little arrows to go back-and-forth between sections sit nice under the big titles.

On the phone it's more of a fight. That tab menu at the top steals a HUGE chunk before I even start, and I gotta scroll and scroll and scroll forever past sliders and code and paragraphs. The green code screen on the phone? Forget it, it's a tiny wall of glowing gibberish, I'd never sit through that on a toilet. The color squares squish down to two-per-row which is fine, still pretty. But overall the phone made me tired and the desktop just made me squint. Both kept that dang tab-wall glued up top and that's the real crime on the little screen.

## My verdict
Look — it's GORGEOUS and it's SMART and it is absolutely, positively not made for a guy with a beer in his hand. The donut and the color candy pulled me in, the big glowy titles and the one obvious UPLOAD button kept me from wandering off immediately — but then it drowned me in slidey bars, gray paragraphs, green code, and a top menu with forty tiny doors. I found the front door (UPLOAD) in two seconds, which saves it. But I'd never explore past it sober, let alone like this. It's a beautiful spaceship cockpit and I just wanted a light switch.

**Score: 5.5 / 10** — on the "would a drunk find the point and stick around" scale. Half a point of gorgeous, half a point of one great big button, minus a lot for making my eyes cross with that tab-wall and all them words.
